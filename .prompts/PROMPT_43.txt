Why: the stream retention policy is a load-bearing design decision. A stream

Assert that `max_deliver` and `5` both appear in the document (or that

Why: without `max_deliver`, a failed message loops forever. The value `5` is the
specific configuration shown in the example. If it were accidentally removed or

**`test_ack_explicit_policy_present`**

Assert that `AckExplicit` appears in the document.

Why: `AckExplicit` is what makes at-least-once delivery safe. If the example
accidentally showed `AckAll` or omitted the ack policy, messages could be dropped

**`test_ack_wait_configured`**

Assert that `ack_wait` appears in the document.

Why: `ack_wait` is the window in which a consumer must ack before NATS redelivers.
Without it, a slow consumer silently loses messages that were redelivered to
another consumer. Its presence in the example verifies the timeout discipline.
